Knee Replacement Recovery Protocol

Fever Guidelines After Surgery

Mild fever below 38°C (100.4°F) may occur during the first 48-72 hours after knee replacement surgery. This is a normal inflammatory response.

Temperature Monitoring:
- Check temperature every 4 hours for first 48 hours
- Document any temperature above 37.5°C (99.5°F)
- Continue monitoring until discharge

When to Contact Doctor:
- Fever exceeds 38°C (100.4°F) after post-op day 3
- Fever higher than 38.5°C (101.3°F) at any time
- Fever accompanied by chills, shaking, or severe headache
- Fever with increased wound redness, swelling, or drainage

Common Causes of Post-op Fever:
1. Normal inflammatory response (days 1-3)
2. Surgical site infection
3. Urinary tract infection  
4. Pneumonia
5. Deep vein thrombosis

Assessment for Fever:
- Check surgical incision for signs of infection
- Assess lungs for clear breath sounds
- Check urinary symptoms
- Evaluate for calf pain or swelling

Treatment:
- Acetaminophen for fever control
- Increased fluid intake
- Cool compresses if needed
- Antibiotics only if infection confirmed
